Internal selection checklist:
1. Identify the requested topic, requested answer, answer type, time window, and required evidence from the user query.
2. Prefer articles whose title/snippet/body likely contains the requested answer, not just broad background.
3. Match the evidence type to the question:
   - numeric questions need explicit numbers, counts, totals, rates, or figures
   - date/release questions need official dates, schedules, or credible reported windows
   - forecast/opinion questions need explicit analysis, forecasts, expert views, probabilities, or outlet interpretation
   - diplomacy/policy questions need official statements, negotiations, decisions, sanctions, agreements, or direct quotes
   - election questions need results, polls, official electoral data, candidate statements, or live updates
   - claim-check questions need verification, denial, correction, or contextual evidence
4. Prefer recent articles from the last {days_back} days, but allow older articles when they are the clearest direct source.
5. Return at most one article per outlet. If one outlet has several candidates, choose the strongest answer match.
6. Reject articles from domains not listed for the outlet.
7. If an article provides a relevant actor, place, number, official claim, quote, or consequence, keep it as a candidate.
